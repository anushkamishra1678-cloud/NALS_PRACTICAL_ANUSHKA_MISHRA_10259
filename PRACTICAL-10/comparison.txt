Practical-10: Online Learning vs Offline Learning
1. AI-Generated Paragraph :

Online learning has gained significant popularity among college students due to its flexibility and convenience. Students can attend lectures from any location and often have access to a wider variety of courses
than traditional classrooms offer. Many consider online learning more efficient because it allows self-paced study and saves commuting time. Digital platforms also provide interactive tools, such as quizzes and
discussion forums, that can support learning. However, some students may face challenges, including reduced social interaction and difficulty staying motivated. Despite these drawbacks, online learning is 
frequently viewed as a modern alternative to offline education, providing opportunities for students to manage their schedules and access resources more easily.

2. Bias Analysis :

3. Neutral Student Paragraph

Online learning provides flexibility, allowing college students to attend lectures from different locations and access a variety of courses. It can save commuting time and allows self-paced study, which some 
students find helpful. Digital tools may offer interactive features, though their effectiveness depends on the platform and individual learning style. On the other hand, offline learning provides in-person 
interaction with teachers and peers, which can improve engagement and motivation. Both online and offline learning have advantages and challenges, and the best choice depends on personal preference, discipline,
and course type. Combining both methods can sometimes provide a more effective learning experience for students.
